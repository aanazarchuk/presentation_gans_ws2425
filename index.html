Introduction to GANs

Generative Adversarial Networks

GANs, or Generative Adversarial Networks, are AI models that generate new, realistic data from existing data.

They were invented by Ian Goodfellow in 2014 and revolutionized artificial intelligence

Why Are GANs Important?

GANs are capable of creating new, high-quality data that looks like real data. Such as:

Creating Photorealistic Images ‚Äì GANs can generate faces of people who don‚Äôt exist.

Generating Synthetic Data ‚Äì Used when real data is limited, GANs can help in training AI models more effectively.

Example: Websites like thispersondoesnotexist.com use GANs to generate fake but realistic human faces.

GANs are helping to push the boundaries of creativity by assisting artists, designers, and content creators.

How GANs Work

GANs consist of two main components:
  • Generator (Generative Model)
  • Discriminator (Discriminative Model)

What is a Generator? üé®

The Generator is a neural network that tries to create realistic data. It takes random noise as input and generates synthetic data. ‚úÖ Purpose: The generator aims to fool the discriminator by producing data that looks like real samples.

üî¢ Mathematical Formula:

The generator G takes noise z as input and outputs a generated data sample (G(z)):

\[ \mathbf{G: z \rightarrow x_{\text{fake}}} \]

This means that the generator maps a noise vector z to a fake sample \( x_{\text{fake}} \) that resembles real data.

What is a Discriminator? üîç

The Discriminator is a neural network that learns to distinguish between real and fake data. It receives both real data from a dataset and synthetic data from the generator.

üéØ Goal: The discriminator learns to identify the difference between real and generated samples.

üìå Mathematical Representation:

The discriminator \( D \) receives a data sample \( x \) and outputs a probability that it is real:

\[ \mathbf{D: x \rightarrow [0,1]} \]

‚úÖ If \( D(x) \approx 1 \) ‚Üí Recognized as real data ‚ùå If \( D(G(z)) \approx 0 \) ‚Üí Recognized as fake data
