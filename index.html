How do GANs work?

Overview

Generator and Discriminator Loss functions

Generator

A network that creates data samples from noise or input. Generator learns to make fakes that look real. ● It learns the probability of features X

Discriminator

A network that distinguishes real data from the fake data generated by the generator. The discriminator is simply a classifier with two classes: real and fake. Given an input x, the discriminator will calculate the probabilities $p(y=\text{real}|x)$ and $p(y=\text{fake}|x)$ and classify $x$. The input $x$ can be anything that you have the generator create and is not limited to images. ● It learns the probability of class Y (real or fake) given features X ● The probabilities are the feedback for the generator

Loss function

For generator -> Ceratinty of discriminator For discriminator -> Did I detect real or fake They learn from the competition with each other ● The generator’s goal is to fool the discriminator ● At the end, fakes look real
